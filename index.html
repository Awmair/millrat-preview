01

Deduction · Word game · Semi-cooperative

Someone’s Y

“A, E, I, O, U … and Someone’s Y!”

Everyone’s a vowel. Except one of you is Y, and trying to blend in. You can only communicate with one-word clues on your turn. But if Y figures you out, they win. Choose your words carefully.

  • 4–6 players
  • 15 min
  • Ages 10+

The aim The vowels work out who Y is before Y works them out.

  1. 1

    Everyone gets a vowel. One player has Y and must blend in.

  2. 2

    Say any one word on your turn. It needn’t contain your vowel; Y can take a token at any time, so catch them in a lie.

  3. 3

    Use tokens to vote for Y. If voted out, Y gets one last chance to guess every other player’s vowel and win.

02

Cooperative · Party game · Unique

Scrapbook

“1000s of old photos finally come in handy!”

Dig through your photo gallery and turn your own memories into clues. Build a collage that hints at the secret card. Can the Guesser crack it?

  • 2–6 players
  • 20 min
  • Ages 12+

The aim Work together to help the Guesser identify a secret card.

  1. 1

    The Guesser holds a Scrapbook card up to the team without looking at it.

  2. 2

    The team scrolls through phone photos. When the Guesser says “Stop,” use the first photo visible.

  3. 3

    Pinch and zoom the photos to recreate something on the card, then let the Guesser identify it.

03

Strategy · Racing · Puzzle

Footfalls

“Mind the gaps to get the scraps!”

Race your rat rivals to the abandoned picnic. Use special abilities to outmaneuver your foes. Stay alert or sink in the roaring waters below!

  • 2–4 players
  • 20 min
  • Ages 10+

The aim Be the rat that reaches the abandoned picnic before your rivals.

  1. 1

    Spend movement points to cross the rickety footbridge, choosing how its tiles connect as you progress.

  2. 2

    Use five special abilities to alter movement, push, sink and block rival rats.

  3. 3

    Mind the gaps and water—the first rat to claim the abandoned picnic wins.

04

Push your luck · Competitive · Take that

Bad Eggs

“Whisk it all!”

Big omelettes score big. But one bad egg might spoil everything. How far will you whisk it?

  • 2–6 players
  • 25 min
  • Ages 10+

The aim Build the biggest omelette without ruining the order.

  1. 1

    Predict dice rolls to set a low Egg Minimum—the lower it is, the easier it is to start scoring.

  2. 2

    Flip egg tokens to crack them open. Multiple yolks help; bad eggs don’t.

  3. 3

    Two bad eggs ruin the omelette. The highest score and biggest omelettes win.
